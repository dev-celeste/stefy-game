# 106.9 Presents: Stefan's Big Day
### A pixel-art choose-your-own-adventure — full design plan (v2 — locked draft)

*A cozy "day in the life" of Stefan that splits into two very different adventures depending on one morning decision. Built to be a personal gift: a single file that runs by double-clicking, works fully offline, and is easy to email or drop on a USB drive.*

---

## 1. The concept in one breath

You wake up as Stefan. It's a normal workday. Penny the cat is already causing trouble, Maria's around, One Piece is paused on the TV, and the radio is tuned to **106.9 M&S** — your and Maria's favorite ridiculous station, playing meme sound stings like it's a real broadcaster. Your work phone buzzes. **Go in to Foulk, or call out?** That single choice sends the whole day down one of two paths, each with its own web of smaller choices and four endings:

- **Branch 1 — "Invasion at Foulk":** You head in to the old folks home with Greg, and an ordinary maintenance day turns strange when it becomes clear some of the *residents aren't human* — a slow-burn alien reveal where Stefan's weird talents (a metal scream, DDR footwork, gamer reflexes, a maintenance man's toolkit) end up saving the day.
- **Branch 2 — "The 106.9 Live Contest":** You stay home for a cozy day with Maria, but the radio announces a live, citywide meme challenge with a dream prize — and the day off becomes a frantic, hilarious quest across Wilmington.

Both branches start from the same cozy morning and are stuffed with personal details so it feels made *just for him*.

## 3. Cast & personal-detail reference sheet

**Stefan (you):** Fair skin, blonde hair, brown eyes, all-black outfit. Maintenance man at **Foulk** (an old folks home), lead singer of Scarpenters by night. Loves video games and anime, films metal covers, from Wilmington, DE, die-hard Philadelphia Eagles fan. Favorite food: pizza + chicken parm. Favorite spot: Nanu's Chicken.

**Greg (best friend / coworker at Foulk):** Goofy and funny, but quick to anger. Running joke: *"Eat yo salad before I toss yo salad."* (Great recurring gag + a big hero-moment payoff.)

**Maria (girlfriend):** They met on a music-lovers' dating app. Currently watching **Attack on Titan** together. Shared inside joke: the fake-but-treated-as-real radio station **106.9 M&S**, which plays funny sounds from viral millennial memes.

**Penny (the tabby cat, he/him):** Sweet but *always* up to something naughty. A chaos cameo in the morning and a stowaway troublemaker in Branch 2.

**Scarpenters (the band):** Stefan sings. Known songs: **"Visions"** and **"Burdened by Weight."** His scream is, canonically, a weapon.

**Flavor to sprinkle throughout:** Kingdom Hearts, Final Fantasy VII, and Dance Dance Revolution references; One Piece playing at home; Eagles gear; August Burns Red & Linkin Park on the speakers; pizza / chicken parm / Nanu's Chicken cravings; the Wilmington, DE setting; and 106.9 M&S meme stings everywhere.

## 5. PROLOGUE — "The Morning" (shared opening)

**Scene: The apartment bedroom → living room.** Morning light. One Piece is paused on the TV, an Eagles pennant on the wall, a mic stand and phone-tripod in the corner (for filming covers). Penny is perched on a shelf, one paw slowly pushing a cup toward the edge. The radio's on **106.9 M&S** — a vine boom plays, then a "bruh" sting, then that classic "OHHH" meme.

> *You're Stefan. The alarm's been snoozed twice. Penny is committing a crime in slow motion. From the kitchen, Maria calls out over a meme sting on the radio. Your work phone buzzes on the nightstand — Foulk needs you today.*

**Small warm-up choice (flavor, doesn't branch):** *Save the cup* / *Let Penny win.* → Either way Penny knocks something else over and you both laugh. Sets the tone.

Then **THE choice:**

- **"Yeah, I'm heading in to Foulk."** → **Branch 1**
- **"Nah… calling out. Let's have a day."** → **Branch 2**

## 6. BRANCH 1 — "Invasion at Foulk"

### 1.0 — Clocking in
You arrive at Foulk. Greg's already there, dramatically eating a salad in the break room.

> Greg: "Yo! You're late. Eat yo salad before I toss yo salad."
> You: "…that's still not how that works, man."

Cozy-mundane setup: there's a busted boiler/AC in the basement and a flickering light in the rec room. But something's *off* today — a few residents are acting strange: one's watching the microwave like a TV, one hasn't blinked in a while, and Mr. Abernathy's hearing aid keeps emitting a weird signal. Then the power surges and a low hum rises from the rec room.

**CHOICE:**
- **A) Go check on the residents / investigate the weirdness.** → 1.A
- **B) Play it safe — radio Greg, grab your tools and gear up first.** → 1.B

### 1.A — Investigate
In the rec room, the "residents" have dropped the act — glowing eyes, a shed layer of disguise, a small craft where the bingo table used to be. Turns out Foulk has been a quiet hideout for a handful of aliens. Greg is already halfway to the exit.

**CHOICE:**
- **A1) Try to talk them down** (Stefan's a performer — maybe he can connect). → toward **Ending 1A (Scream)** or **1C (The Reveal)**
- **A2) Grab a wrench and get ready to fight.** → toward **Ending 1B (Co-op)**

### 1.B — Tool up & fortify
You and Greg barricade the rec-room doors and rig something from the maintenance closet (a maintenance man improvises). Through the window you count more of them now, scanning the halls with strange light.

**CHOICE:**
- **B1) Rig a homemade "sound cannon"** using the PA system + your mic. → **Ending 1A (Scream)**
- **B2) Sneak the real residents out the side and hide until it blows over.** → **Ending 1D (Escape)**

### Branch 1 Endings

**Ending 1A — "Visions (Live at Foulk)."** Stefan grabs the mic, patches into the PA, cues *"Visions,"* and unleashes a scream so pure it overloads the aliens' tech — windows rattle, their systems fry, and they retreat. Greg, tearing up: *"Bro… that was the best set you've ever done."* *(Hero ending — the metal-scream-as-weapon payoff.)*

**Ending 1B — "Co-op Mode."** You and Greg fight them off like a two-player boss battle — you call combos ("left! roll! now!") on pure gamer instinct, Greg swings the pipe wrench. Teamwork wins. Fist-bump over the wreckage. Greg: *"NOW that's how you toss a salad."* *(Friendship ending.)*

**Ending 1C — "The Reveal (they just wanted to retire)."** Instead of fighting, Stefan talks to them — and learns the aliens weren't invading. They picked Foulk to quietly live out their days in peace, and got spooked. Stefan's kindness (and a mic-drop rendition of a song) wins them over; they stay on as the home's most interesting residents, and it becomes his and Greg's secret. *(Sweet-twist ending, adapted to the residents-are-aliens concept.)*

**Ending 1D — "Lived to Tell It."** You and Greg hunker down in the supply closet with the real residents, eating cold pizza until it all quiets down. You never fully learn what happened. You clock out shaken and order Nanu's Chicken. Best worst day ever. *(Cozy-survivor ending.)*

## 7. BRANCH 2 — "The 106.9 Live Contest" (the Riddle Hunt)

**Design theme:** the perfect mirror of Branch 1. Where Branch 1 uses Stefan's *body/skills* (scream, handiness), Branch 2 uses his *brain and passions* (fandom knowledge, DDR feet). Each challenge is keyed to something that makes Stefan *Stefan*, so winning feels earned and personal. Maria is his co-solver (they met over exactly this stuff); Penny is the chaos wildcard.

### 2.0 — The hunt begins
You call out. Maria's thrilled. You settle in for an **Attack on Titan** marathon with 106.9 M&S on in the background. Then the DJ cuts in — LIVE:

> DJ (over an airhorn sting): "The 106.9 M&S RIDDLE HUNT starts NOW! Crack our clues, race across Wilmington, first to the finish wins the GRAND PRIZE — YOUR band opens for AUGUST BURNS RED!"

Stefan freezes. *Scarpenters. Opening for August Burns Red.* This is winnable. Penny's ears perk up too. The first clue drops on air.

### Challenge 1 — The Riddle (game/anime brain)
The DJ reads a cryptic clue stuffed with **Kingdom Hearts / One Piece** references that decodes to a real Wilmington location.

**CHOICE — how do you crack it?**
- **A) Stefan's gamer instinct** (the Kingdom Hearts read). → solo-hero path (toward **2A**)
- **B) Maria's anime angle** (the One Piece read). → team path (toward **2B**)

*(Both are correct — this sets the tone/flavor and nudges which ending you land.)*

### Challenge 2 — The DDR Rhythm Gate (playable minigame)
The clue leads to a barcade with a **DDR machine**. To grab the next clue you must clear a song on the real, keyboard-playable minigame (see §8a).

**CHOICE — how do you play it?**
- **A) Go for a flawless high-difficulty run** (showman, risky — harder chart). → **2A**
- **B) Co-op it with Maria / ham it up** (easier chart, funnier). → **2B / 2C**
- *(Wildcard: Penny escapes onto the dance pad mid-song — depending on how it goes, can tip you toward **2D**.)*

### Finale — The last dash
How you played decides the ending (mirrors Branch 1's four-lever design):

### Branch 2 Endings

**Ending 2A — "Champions of 106.9."** Gamer instinct + a flawless DDR run: you win solo-hero style. Weeks later Scarpenters is on stage opening for **August Burns Red**, Maria front row, Stefan screaming *"Burdened by Weight"* to a real crowd. *(Triumphant, deeply personal.)*

**Ending 2B — "Two of Us."** You leaned on Maria's anime smarts and co-op'd the dance — you win *as a team*, a callback to how they met on the music app. Scarpenters gets the gig, together. *(Heartfelt-triumphant.)*

**Ending 2C — "Second Place, Best Day."** You chased the funniest option all day (Nanu's Chicken detour, chaos, Penny's escapes) and lose the prize by a hair — but it was the perfect day off. *(Sweet.)*

**Ending 2D — "Penny Goes Viral."** Penny's DDR-pad stunt blows up so hard that 106.9 crowns *the cat* the winner and makes him the station mascot. Scarpenters still gets the gig, per Penny's "management." *(Funniest — Penny is the real MVP.)*

## 8a. The DDR minigame (Branch 2 centerpiece) ✅

A simple, faithful, keyboard-playable rhythm game — the interactive heart of Branch 2.

- **How it plays:** arrows scroll upward in time with the music toward a target line at the top. Player presses the matching **↑ ↓ ← → arrow keys** (WASD also supported) as each arrow crosses the line.
- **Timing judgments:** Perfect / Good / Miss, based on how close the keypress is to the target. Feeds a **combo counter** and a **score/energy bar**.
- **Clear condition:** reach a score threshold (or keep the energy bar above empty) to "clear" the song and unlock the next clue. Fail = retry (low-stakes, it's a gift).
- **Difficulty maps to the choices:** the "flawless solo run" is a harder, faster chart; the "co-op with Maria" is an easier, funnier chart. Same engine, different note pattern.
- **Penny wildcard:** a scripted moment where Penny darts across the pad, throwing a couple of surprise arrows — comedic, and can nudge the ending.
- **Song:** a Scarpenters chiptune ("Visions" or "Burdened by Weight" as an 8-bit arrangement) synced to the note chart.
- **Fully offline & self-contained**, keyboard only, no assets required beyond the note chart data.

## 8. Sound & music (retro Pokémon/GBA feel) ✅

Fully doable and fully offline. Plan:

- **Looping chiptune background music** that changes by context: a warm, sleepy **apartment theme**; a tense, pulsing **"something's wrong at Foulk" theme**; an upbeat **contest/chase theme**; and a triumphant **ending jingle**.
- **Sound effects:** menu-move blip, choice-confirm chime, retro **text-blip** as dialogue types out, a page/scene-transition swoosh, and a **stinger** when the invasion or contest kicks off.
- **106.9 M&S meme stings** used intentionally as SFX at big beats (airhorn, "OHHH," vine boom).
- **A mute / volume toggle** always visible.
- **Build approach:** start the prototype with lightweight **in-code Web Audio** beeps/SFX (zero extra files, instantly retro), then layer in richer looping music tracks (embedded so it all still runs from one folder / USB).

## 10. How we'll build it (technical plan)

- **One self-contained HTML file** (plus an `images/` and small `audio/` folder, or fully embedded), so it runs by double-clicking in any browser — no install, no internet. Ideal for emailing or a USB gift.
- The **story lives in a simple, readable data structure** (each scene = an ID, text, a background, a character, music cue, and its choices). Editing the story later is about as easy as editing Twine passages — no need to touch game code.
- **Crisp pixel rendering** so art stays sharp and blocky.
- **Hybrid interactive touches** (Penny darting across the screen, walking to a spot, a simple "boss meter" during the Foulk climax) layered on top of the illustrated scenes.
- **Art & audio pipeline:** drop a PNG or sound into its folder, reference the filename in the scene data, done. Start with placeholders, swap in your Procreate art and final music anytime.

3. Polish pass: swap in your Procreate art, add richer looping music, final SFX, title screen (106.9 M&S branded).
